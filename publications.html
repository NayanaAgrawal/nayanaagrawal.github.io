Cerita Rakyat Dunia

(Folk Tales of the World)

I started this project in September, 2017. My goal was to create a compilation of stories for young children in our local community in Jakarta, Indonesia. I wanted to produce something which would be educational and still enjoyable, which is why I decided to make the book bilingual. It provided the children opportunities to learn English as well as read in the local language, Bahasa Indonesia.

The first stage was to select group members to help me with the project. I selected people who were punctual, responsible, and would be passionate about the project because it was volunteer-based and required a high amount of effort. The people on the team were divided into three categories: writers, illustrators, and translators.

We collaborated to put together drafts of the story. I was responsible for creating deadlines, providing file sizes and other guidelines, and writing one of the stories within the book. After we compiled all the material, I assembled the project into a legible book. I made the book cover by making a mosaic of all the art styles within, and a table of contents to organize the text better.

One of my goals since the start of the project was to distribute the final product for free to surrounding communities and libraries that were in need of new material. Because of the size of the project and the cost of printing multiple copies in colour, we requested funding from the PTA. After our presentation, we were given more funds than we applied for because the board members approved highly of the idea and wanted us to publish a book of the best possible quality.

I contacted a local publishing agency, pitched our project, and agreed on a cost. Throughout the next few weeks, we discussed paper quality, ink costs, binding methods, and other details involved in publishing a book.

At the end of the project, we were able to print and distribute 60 copies of the book to the local community. I learned many valuable skills through this experience. Managing a project with so many moving components was challenging and taught me the importance of planning ahead, setting deadlines, and co-ordinating efforts between the teams.
